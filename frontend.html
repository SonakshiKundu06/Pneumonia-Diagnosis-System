What is Pneumonia?

Pneumonia is an infection that irritates the air sacs of one or both lungs. The air sacs can become filled with pus or fluid, presenting symptoms like phlegm or pus with cough, dyspnea and chills and fever. It may be due to bacteria, viruses, or fungi and can be either mild or life-threatening. Early detection is essential for proper treatment.

Symptoms

  • Cough, which may produce phlegm
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Shortness of breath
  • Coughing or Chest pain when breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Confusion, especially in older adults

Cure and Treatment

The treatment for pneumonia depends on the type and severity of the infection. Common treatments include:

  • Antibiotics for bacterial pneumonia
  • Antiviral medications for viral pneumonia
  • Over-the-counter medications to relieve symptoms
  • Rest and hydration
  • In severe cases, hospitalization may be required for oxygen therapy or intravenous antibiotics
